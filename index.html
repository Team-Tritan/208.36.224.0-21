208.36.224.0/21 / AS393577

This page is regarding the IPv4 subnet 208.36.224.0/21, which was historically pointed to a Road Runner ASN in the early 2000s. Over the years, due to changes in ownership and multiple acquisitions, this subnet has been lost in bureaucracy, allowing us to announce it.

Our organization provides community-based ISP services, and we plan to utilize this IPv4 subnet to support our community while we grow. Despite its historical association with Road Runner, it has not been actively used since 2008. Since the acquisition of Road Runner and subsequent changes in ownership, this subnet has been sitting still, pointing to our ASN.

We would like to emphasize our intention and request to continue to be allowed to announce this subnet, as it aligns with our commitment to providing reliable internet services to the BGP hobbyist community we serve. As we plan to utilize more than it has been used in the last 15 years during the time of writing, we appreciate your understanding and cooperation in this matter.

The Downfalls of IPv4 Hogging

IPv4 hogging, where organizations hold onto more IP addresses than they need, contributes to the exhaustion of available IPv4 addresses. This practice limits the pool of addresses available for new networks, hindering the growth of the internet and forcing the adoption of IPv6.

As we navigate through the bureaucracy of our current digital landscape for ISPs, it's crucial to address these issues and ensure efficient allocation of IP resources for a sustainable and scalable internet infrastructure.
